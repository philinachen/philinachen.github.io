MSAE Spring Golf Outing

Michigan Society of Association Executive(MSAE)’s Spring Golf Outing is an annual event aiming at providing members opportunities to network and engage with their peers in the industry. For this project, I wanted to break audiences’ stereotypes about MSAE, which is very professional but lack of flexibility. Therefore, I applied the retro style and vivid colors including orange and blue to the cover. I wanted audiences to regard it as a fun, compelling event that they would want to go out and play golf when they see the magnified golf ball popping out in front of their eyes.

MSAE 15th Annual Diamond Awards

Michigan Society of Association Executive(MSAE)’s Diamond Awards were created to recognize outstanding achievements in the association industry. This year is its 15th convention. The 3D diamond on the cover was inspired from the geometric shapes inside the trophy of the Diamond Awards, which were generated by reflections from different facets. Red is the theme color of the Diamond Awards. Additionally, according to marriage.about.com, ruby red is the 15th traditional wedding anniversary colors. Like the geometric shapes that were delicately placed inside the trophy, this design was expected to be elegant, clean and impressive.

Philina Chen Personal Website v2.0

Visit the Site

This is the first version of my personal website (I redesigned my website that you are currently viewing for better readability). It is a responsive, one-page scrolling, animation-based website. Three geometric shapes - triangles, circles, and rectangles – take the major responsibility of maintaining consistency of the website. I love clean design as well as snow mountains. The image of the peak of the snow mountain could be interpreted as the other form of triangles. The blue circle represents the sky over the mountain, which soon becomes a yellow sun when scrolling down. Those nested rectangles symbolize the comfort zones in my life. To reach the peak, it’s necessary to stepping out those comfort zones. Also, if you could expand your comfort zones, which means you are more open to challenges, you would be one step closer to the peak. That is what I am striving for.

MSAE ORGPRO 2016

Michigan Society of Association Executive(MSAE)’s ORGPRO is Michigan’s premier education and networking convention for professionals in the not-for-profit industry. The two characters “TC” derive from ORGPRO 2016’s slogan - “True Commitment,” and its host city - Traverse City. Cyan was chosen as the theme color because Traverse City is known by its amazing view of Lake Michigan and the blue sky. Instead of applying drop shadows to “TC,” I replaced them with white lines, which suggests the extended networking and educational opportunities that MSAE wants to provide for professionals.

Online Dating Infographic

This infographic was created when I took a course about online interpersonal relationships. Nowadays online dating has become the third most popular way for single people to build relationships. It was interesting to find out why people trust online dating and how to succeed in such relationships.

“Love is a maze” – it is the concept of the design. According to Mark Knapp’s relationship model, generally people go through the process of initialization, experimentation, intensifying, integration, and bonding to achieve an idea relationship. This process is like walking in a maze: sometimes people get lost; sometimes they find the way. In the infographic, each section is connected with a path of the maze, which is not the shortest path to arrive to the next section. However, they all finally arrive, symbolizing the arrival of each stage in a relationship.

Jakaa Carpool App

Jakaa carpool app was a group project we did for our HCI experience design course. I participated the user research and was responsible for designing the prototype and making it interactive. The app was designed for the people who seek for a reliable, safe, and inexpensive way to carpool within their communities. People are able to communicate with drivers and other passengers directly using Jakaa’s in-built Community function. Additionally, Jakaa aims to promote ecolife by encouraging people to carpool more. Passengers are not required to pay for a ride unless they choose to send tips to drivers. They would also earn coupons based on their carpool histories.

Design Procedure

Logo Design
Green is a symbolic color for ecolife, so I chose light teal as the them color. The icon can be view as the first character of Jakaa - J, as well as a leaf that is cut by half, which also symbolizes ecolife.

More About the App
Instead of a sidebar, a tab bar was chosen to improve usability. User are able to access major functions directly from the tab bar. It is composed of five sections: communities, friends, rewards, settings and a carpool searching shortcut, which is highlighted by a green circle.

Unlike the majority of current ridesharing apps, Jakaa provides user the option to choose a driver before getting a ride. Because through our interviews with target audiences, we noticed security is the primary concern of most of passengers. Being able to review drivers’ profiles and choose a driver make user more comfortable and confident when requesting a ride. The driver profile shows the chosen driver’s basic information, vehicle information, the amount of people currently in the car, communities that the driver joins, awards that the driver receives, and reviews and ratings given by other passengers.
